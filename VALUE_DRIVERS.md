# WildcatIQ - Value Drivers

Why operators buy, in priority order. This is the narrative spine for landing-page
copy and positioning. Read it top-down: the thesis is the product; everything else
is an expression of it.

---

## The thesis (the only driver that matters)

**WildcatIQ unifies every part of an oil & gas operation into one context layer, then
runs AI on top of it.**

That's the whole product. Not four AI features bolted onto four modules - **one AI
reasoning over one unified model of the business**. Exploration, production,
operations, and finance stop being silos in separate tools and become a single
context an assistant can reason across.

The modules (Wells, Accounting, Files, People, …) are just *where the data enters*.
The value is the **connective tissue between them** - and that tissue is something no
point solution can replicate, because no point solution can see across the silos.

Every driver below is the same engine, pointed at a different part of the business.

---

## Why this is uniquely valuable

A standalone production tracker, a standalone accounting tool, a standalone log
viewer, and a generic chatbot are all commodities. The moat is the **compound**:

- **One context** - production, revenue, NRI, posted prices, equipment, documents,
  and well logs all reference each other.
- **AI grounded in *your* data** - RAG over structured records *and* unstructured
  documents (OCR + embeddings), not a generic model guessing.
- **Cross-domain reasoning** - the assistant can answer questions that span domains
  in a single thought.

Remove any one of these and the value collapses to "another tool."

---

## The apex: cross-domain reasoning

The highest-value capability isn't any single domain getting better in isolation -
it's the questions that **only become answerable once everything shares one context**:

> "Which wells logged good porosity but are underproducing their offsets, *and* are
> losing money, *and* are overdue for a workover I haven't scheduled?"

That question touches exploration, production, finance, and operations at once. No
siloed tool can answer it - not because the data doesn't exist, but because it lives
in four disconnected systems. WildcatIQ answers it because the context layer
dissolves the boundaries.

This is the real meaning of *"run your entire company with AI."*

## The same engine, by domain

The thesis applies everywhere. Each domain is better because it's reasoned over
*in context with the others*, not on its own.

### Exploration / subsurface - the highest ceiling
The biggest-dollar decisions in the business meet the scarcest, most expensive
expertise - which is exactly where AI-over-context has the most leverage.

- **The tell: WildcatIQ reads the LAS file.** Well-log interpretation
  (net pay, porosity, water saturation, formation tops, perforation intervals) is
  specialist petrophysicist/geologist work - today that means a $150k+/yr hire you
  can't justify, per-project consultants, or $20k–$80k desktop software. The platform
  renders *and indexes* the log, so the AI can read the curves.
- **Bypassed-pay hunting** across a legacy well library - re-reading decades-old logs
  against current economics is finding money in assets you already own.
- **Log-to-production feedback** - "this zone logged 12% porosity but the well
  underproduced its offsets → recompletion candidate." Impossible without the log and
  the accounting in the same context.
- **Offset correlation & acquisition due diligence** - read a whole package's logs and
  production history in an afternoon instead of weeks.

A single bypassed-pay recompletion or an avoided dry hole can dwarf a year of
back-office savings. **Lead with this as the proof point.**

### Per-well financial clarity - high frequency, high stakes
Revenue, lifting cost, and P/L per well; posted-price derivation; benchmark-vs-realized
pricing; cash-flow trends. Answers the question operators care about most - *which
wells make money* - which drives the highest-leverage operating decisions (work over,
plug, invest). Most small operators don't know this with confidence today.

### Back-office automation - felt most often
AI statement extraction (PDF/image/CSV → drafted transactions, dated to production
month, matched to the chart of accounts and wells) deletes the most tedious,
error-prone job in the office. Plus OCR-indexing on every upload and autosave
everywhere. The most viscerally felt time-savings, every single day.

### Royalty / interest-owner management - risk reduction
Decimal interest, NRI, distributions to interest owners, last-payment tracking -
calculated from the same revenue data. Financially and legally critical, and a classic
source of costly errors (mispaid owners → disputes, liability).

### Operations coordination - the connective baseline
Wells, equipment, tasks, calendar, inventory, and people, tied to the wells they
actually concern. On its own this is table stakes; *in context* it's what lets the AI
connect "underproducing well" to "overdue workover task."

## How the drivers rank

| Driver | Frequency | $ per decision | Expertise replaced |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Exploration / subsurface** | Lower | **Highest** | **Scarcest** (petrophysicist/geologist) |
| **Per-well financial clarity** | High | High | Analyst / accountant |
| **Back-office automation** | Highest | Low per event | Bookkeeper |
| **Royalty / owner management** | Medium | High (risk) | Land / revenue accountant |
| **Operations coordination** | High | Low | Coordinator (table stakes) |

- **Highest ceiling:** exploration - one decision can be worth more than everything
  else combined.
- **Highest frequency:** production + finance - every well, every month.
- **Both are downstream of the same engine.** The per-domain wins are *expressions* of
  the thesis, not separate value props.

## Positioning guidance (for the landing page)

1. **Lead with the thesis** - unify the whole business into one context, put AI on top.
   This is the hook, not a feature list.
2. **Prove it with exploration first** - "Orion reads your well logs and finds bypassed
   pay" is sharper and more differentiated than "auto-import your statements," even
   though the latter is felt more often. Lead with the ceiling.
3. **Support with frequency** - know per-well P/L, auto-import statements, pay owners
   correctly. These make the claim concrete and believable.
4. **Land the apex** - show a cross-domain question no other tool can answer. That's
   the moment the "entire company" claim becomes undeniable.
5. **Frame modules as data entry, not the product** - the product is the context layer
   and the AI; the modules are where the data comes from.

**Don't** sell it as a suite of separate AI features. The differentiation is the
connective tissue - say so.
